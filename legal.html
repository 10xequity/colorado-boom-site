Section 1

Privacy Policy

Colorado Boom Volleyball Club ("Colorado Boom," "we," "us") respects the privacy of the families and athletes we serve. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. It applies to coloradoboom.com and to registrations submitted through forms we link to.

Information we collect

We collect information you choose to provide when you register for tryouts, open gyms, training, or programs, or when you contact us. Depending on the form, this may include:

  • Athlete and parent/guardian names and contact details (email, phone, mailing address);
  • Athlete date of birth, grade, or age division for placement;
  • Emergency contact and basic health/medical notes where required for participation;
  • Program preferences and the content of messages you send us.

Our registration and sign-up forms are hosted by third-party services (such as Google Forms). Information entered into those forms is collected and stored on those providers' systems under their terms and privacy policies. We do not collect payment card numbers on this website.

How we use information

  • To process registrations and manage participation in our programs;
  • To communicate about schedules, tryouts, placements, billing, and club news;
  • To support athlete safety and emergency response;
  • To meet obligations to our sanctioning bodies (Rocky Mountain Region and USA Volleyball) and to comply with law.

How we share information

We do not sell personal information. We share it only as needed to operate the club: with service providers that host our forms or email, and with our sanctioning bodies (RMR/USAV) as required for membership, registration, and competition. We may disclose information when required by law or to protect the safety of participants.

Children's privacy

Our programs serve minors, and registration is completed by a parent or legal guardian. We collect a child's information only through a parent or guardian for the purpose of participation. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 13 without parental involvement. Parents and guardians may review, update, or request deletion of their child's information by contacting us.

Cookies and analytics

This website is intended to function without requiring tracking cookies. Where we use basic, privacy-respecting measurement to understand site traffic, it is configured to avoid collecting personally identifying information. Forms and embedded content provided by third parties may set their own cookies under their policies.

Data retention and your choices

We keep registration and participation records for as long as needed to run the club and meet legal and sanctioning requirements, then dispose of them. You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your information, and you may unsubscribe from club emails at any time.

Contact

Questions or requests about privacy: admin@coloradoboom.com.

This Privacy Policy is provided as general information for families and is not legal advice. Colorado Boom should have it reviewed by qualified counsel before relying on it.

Section 3

Accessibility Statement

Colorado Boom Volleyball Club is committed to making coloradoboom.com usable for everyone, including people who rely on assistive technologies.

Our standard

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. This includes goals such as sufficient color contrast, keyboard-navigable controls, descriptive alternative text for images, and a logical, semantic page structure.

Ongoing effort

Accessibility is an ongoing process. As we add content and features, we work to meet these standards, and we welcome feedback that helps us improve. Some third-party content embedded on the site (such as forms, maps, or social feeds) may not be fully under our control.

Report a barrier

If you encounter a barrier or need information in an alternative format, contact admin@coloradoboom.com with the page address and a description of the problem, and we will work to provide the information or fix the issue.

This statement describes our goals and good-faith efforts and is not a guarantee or legal advice. For ADA compliance specifics, consult qualified counsel.
