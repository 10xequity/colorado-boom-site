Financial Aid Opportunities

We never want cost to be the only thing between an athlete and the court. Start with the Rocky Mountain Region, which offers need-based financial assistance to qualifying families across the region.

Families can also apply for club-sports scholarships through Chance Sports, a nonprofit that helps young athletes access club programs. A third option, the Colorado Athletics Foundation, works to expand access to youth athletics across Colorado. Aid through any of these sources is not guaranteed — awards are reviewed and granted on each organization's own criteria. Questions? Email admin@coloradoboom.com.

Making the Team & Season Commitment

What a roster spot means — team size, the season-long commitment, and how court time works.

Teams carry 10–12 players. Smaller rosters mean more reps in practice and more meaningful court time across a season than larger squads allow.

Committing to a team is a season-long commitment, and families are financially responsible for the full club season once they accept a spot. The one exception is a season-ending injury, in which case remaining dues may be prorated. We ask families to weigh the commitment carefully during the offer window — the OCS gives you time to decide before you sign.

No — match playing time isn't guaranteed or split evenly, but court time at Colorado Boom goes well beyond game day. Our commitment is to touches and reps: three practices a week, with Fridays focused on scrimmages and position work, plus Boomtown drop-in included in your club fee for extra play. Girls are also invited to the summer grass program at no added cost. In matches, coaches give each athlete as much court time as possible, weighed against skill level, the matchup, position need, attitude — and practice attendance, which is a key factor in game-time decisions. More on how playing time and team logistics work will be covered at the introductory parent meeting at the facility at the start of the club season.

Yes — if a team has an open spot that fits her age group and ability. Reach out to admin@coloradoboom.com and we'll let you know what's available.

Volleyball Rules

New to the sport? Here's enough to follow the action — the full guide has scoring, every common fault, and a glossary.

Rally scoring — a point on every serve. Matches are best-of-five; sets are played to 25 (win by two) and a deciding fifth set to 15. Junior pool play is often best two-of-three.

Three per side before the ball must cross the net — a block doesn't count as one of the three.

Net touches, lifts/carries, double contacts, foot faults on the serve, rotation overlaps, and illegal back-row attacks. The full guide explains each one.

Volleyball Positions & Rotations

Who does what on the court — with rotations, court dimensions, and net heights on the full page.

Setter, outside (left-side) hitter, opposite (right-side) hitter, middle blocker, libero, and defensive specialist. Teams usually run a 5-1 or 6-2 system by age and level.

A back-row defensive specialist in a contrasting jersey who substitutes freely without counting against team subs, and can't attack a ball entirely above the net or block.

On winning back the serve, players rotate one spot clockwise; everyone must be in correct order at the moment of serve. The full page has a rotation diagram and a short video.

Good Sideline Behavior

A quick guide to being the parent every coach and player wants on the sideline.

Cheer effort over outcome, let the coaches coach, and be a calm, positive presence after tough matches. Your athlete plays better knowing home is a judgment-free zone.

If a match or decision raises strong emotions, wait 24 hours before contacting a coach. It keeps conversations productive and models composure for the players.

Support all players positively, respect officials and opposing teams, and leave the coaching to the staff. No sideline officiating or coaching from the stands.

Start with an email to admin@coloradoboom.com. We follow a clear chain of communication so every concern reaches the right person.

Arrive on time, keep up with fees and commitments, and pitch in at tournaments (work/volunteer shifts). A strong team culture starts with engaged families.

RMR Essentials for Parents

The region-specific answers families ask about most.

The region's regular-season series — roughly six one-day events, usually Sundays at assigned sites across Colorado. Schedules and court assignments post on TM2/AES by the Wednesday before, and match play typically starts at 8:00 AM.

Most RMR events charge about $10 per person per day at the gate (plus a small processing fee), with discounts on multi-day events. Passes are per person — bring everyone attending.

Yes, through a defined process using TruVolley rankings and RMR guidelines. In the youth league (RYL) a team may play up for the full season. Talk with your director before committing.

New players can start with a low-cost RYL/tryout membership (about $20) and upgrade to a full-season membership when they sign with a club. Prior-season members stay valid through Aug 31, so no extra tryout membership is needed.

Players who are 18 (or turn 18 before Aug 31 of the season) must complete USAV SafeSport certification. All coaches and directors are SafeSport-trained and background-checked.

Some larger tournaments require booking lodging through the event's official housing partner to participate. Always check an event's rules before booking travel.

Schedules and court assignments post on TM2/AES a few days before each event; live scoring runs digitally on-site; season rankings are published by the RMR.

Got an Offer? The RMR OCS

How club offers and commitments work after tryouts.

The RMR Online Commitment System is how clubs send offers and how families accept them. It's run separately from USAV/RMR membership — you'll still join, sign waivers, and pay dues after committing.

Offers go out no earlier than 10:00 AM on July 12 (athletes playing 15U–18U) and 10:00 AM on Sept 6 (11U–14U). Clubs keep each offer open for at least 48 hours.

By email from offers@rmr.vstarvolleyball.com. Add that address to your contacts now so the offer doesn't get filtered into spam. When the offer arrives, review and sign the Terms & Conditions box in addition to accepting.

Once you accept through the OCS, your athlete is committed to that club for the season and can't try out for or play with another club. Take the full window to weigh your options before committing.
