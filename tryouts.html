What to Expect

  • What to bring: knee pads, court shoes (no running shoes), a water bottle, and athletic clothing.
  • Evaluation: coaches assess technical skill, court awareness, coachability, and athleticism — not just experience.
  • Process: skills assessment, then game-simulation scrimmages and wash drills under pressure.
  • Placement: directors and lead coaches finalize rosters following RMR offer and commitment guidelines; decisions within two weeks of the final session.
  • All skill levels welcome — including players new to club volleyball.

How to Prepare for Tryouts

A few weeks of focused prep makes a real difference — here's where to put the time.

Sharpen the core skills

  • Passing: serve-receive reps against a wall or a partner — platform angle and footwork matter more than power.
  • Serving: consistency over speed. Aim to land 8 of 10 serves in, then add zones.
  • Setting & ball control: wall-sets and pepper build the soft hands coaches look for.

Build volleyball-specific fitness

  • Jump & land: approach jumps and box step-downs train explosive takeoff and safe landings.
  • Lateral speed: short shuffle and reaction drills carry straight into defense.
  • Core & shoulders: planks and light band work protect against the most common volleyball injuries.

Know the game

  • Learn the positions and rotations and the basic rules and faults — players who understand the game stand out.
  • Watch high-level matches and track one position to see how it moves.

On tryout day

  • Arrive early, hydrated, in court shoes and knee pads. Warm up before you walk in.
  • Coaches weigh effort, coachability, and communication as heavily as raw skill — bring energy and a good attitude.

Got an Offer? How the RMR OCS Works

After tryouts, club offers come through the Rocky Mountain Region's Online Commitment System (“the Portal”).

  • Offers arrive by email from offers@rmr.vstarvolleyball.com — add it to your contacts now so it doesn't land in spam.
  • Offers go out no earlier than 10am, July 12 (15U–18U) and 10am, Sept 6 (11U–14U). Clubs keep each offer open at least 48 hours.
  • Accepting an offer commits your athlete to that club for the season — they can't try out for or play with another club after that.
  • The OCS is separate from membership: you'll still join USAV/RMR, sign waivers, and pay dues.
